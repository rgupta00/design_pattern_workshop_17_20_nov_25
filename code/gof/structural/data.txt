Muhammad Ali was an American professional boxer, born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr.
He is widely regarded as one of the greatest heavyweight boxers of all time.
Ali won the Olympic gold medal in 1960.
He became the world heavyweight champion three times.
His boxing style combined speed, footwork, and charisma — “Float like a butterfly, sting like a bee.”
Ali was known for his confidence, poetry, and psychological tactics in the ring.
He refused to fight in the Vietnam War, leading to a ban from boxing for several years.
His famous fights include “Rumble in the Jungle” and “Thrilla in Manila.”
Outside boxing, he was a global humanitarian and civil rights advocate.
Muhammad Ali remains an iconic figure who inspired millions through sport and courage.
